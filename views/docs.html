ARIVU

OVERVIEW

Arivu is a student-led initiative designed to encourage students to get started with their projects or join in on other incredible projects.

GOALS

Offer students the chance to address their inquiries regarding any topic of their interest. Essentially, eliminates all barriers that hinder students from sharing their ideas with the world, and facilitates their learning through a project-based approach. The project allows students to exercise their creativity and explore the vast resources of the internet, allowing them to discover new and interesting technologies that they are passionate about learning.

SPECIFICATIONS

Each student is provided with the basic information on where to get started, Afterwards, the student is left to his creativity and interest in the project. An aspect that motivates the participant is that once the project is complete they can flaunt it in their resume and can act as a project they did on their own, and motivate them to do many more and also help them acclimatize themselves with doing projects.

PROCEDURE

The sessions aim to provide a platform for students who are interested in any of the topics provided by the mentors as well as engage with mentors. Its purpose is to allow students to seek clarification on doubts they might have, or create interest in something new they want to explore. Participants who are intrigued by the prospect can also undertake a project of their choosing. They will be assigned deadlines and dates for project status checks. All projects that have been completed will be featured on a website for one year. Throughout this period, participants are encouraged to look up the answers themselves or in case of despair reach out to resource persons for doubt clarification. The project component accommodates a maximum of 25 projects. To secure a spot in the project as a mentor, .It's important to note that the open session is open to all (as you would imagine), free of charge and welcomes any individuals who express interest. Those eager to partake in the project are invited to submit a project abstract, expressing their interest and aspirations.

OUTCOME

Through an evaluation of the project abstracts, a curated collection of projects will be chosen, prioritizing their feasibility. Once selected the students will be allotted project slots to work on their projects, or collectively as a group. These projects will be monitored and their status will be checked. On the successful completion of the project, these projects will be on display for the entire internet for a whole year. The deadlines for the projects will be allotted according to the complexity of the projects.
